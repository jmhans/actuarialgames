Monday Morning Quarterbacks Fantasy Football Keeper League Constitution (updated 18/517/2015)

1       General Constitution Guidelines

1.1  Constitutional Application Statement

The statements herein are intended to communicate the intent of the rules that govern the league.  It is entirely plausible that interpretation of the statements herein is unclear and imperfect at best.  In the event of disagreement in interpretation of constitutional statements, the commissioner has authority to remedy using the following course(s) of action (Order to be followed precisely until resolution is achieved):

1)       Propose a ruling and obtain mutual agreement of all acutely affected parties.

2)       Stage a league-wide vote to amend the constitution.

a.        During a season (between keeper lock date and championship game): Refund fees to offended party and reduce league payouts accordingly. (Note: refund action will likely result in dismissal from the league for future seasons).

b.       Between seasons: Remove owner(s) from the league to ensure cohesion for future seasons. 

2       League Description & Overview

2.1  League structure

2.1.1          League Size

                                The league will consist of 10 teams. 

2.1.2          Division Structure

                                Teams will be split into two divisions for the purpose of determining playoff qualifiers and winnings. 

2.1.2.1                Division Draft

After keeper selections have locked, divisions will be re-established using a draft-style selection as described in the below process:

1)       The “Picking Team” is defined as the last place finisher from the prior season (including playoff results). 

2)       The “On Deck Team” is defined as the next to last place finisher from the prior season (including playoff results). 

3)       The Picking Team will select one team from the pool of remaining teams not including those previously picked or the current “On Deck Team” to be included in his division.

4)       The On Deck Team will then become the Picking Team, and the team chosen in Step 3 will become the On Deck Team.

5)    Repeat steps 3 and 4 until all teams have been assigned to a division. 

2.2  League Finances

2.2.1          Fees

2.2.1.1                Entry Fee

The entry fee will be $40.  Additionally, if the league decides at the draft to use a website that has an associated fee, the cost will be split between all teams equally.

2.2.1.2                Free Agent Transaction Costs

Free Agent transactions have no actual cost.  (There may be fantasy auction budget cost). 

2.2.1.3                Trade Transaction Costs

Trades with other owners have no actual cost. 

2.2.2          Payouts

Payout will total 100% of entry fees.  With 10 teams, the total pool is $400.  Detailed payout information is shown in the table below.

2.3  Changes to league Rules

2.3.1          Pre-Season Changes

Anything in these rules can be changed prior to the keeper lock date for any season by a 60% majority of the owners (6 for a 10 team league).  If an owner abstains from a vote on a proposal, his/her vote will effectively be cast as a vote against the proposal. 

2.3.2          In-Season Changes

Any changes in the rules during a season that will be applied for the remainder of that season (i.e scoring, payouts, playoff setup, Free Agency rules) must be approved by all active owners.

 

The date for “in-season” changes will be synchronized with the keeper lock date.

2.4  Owner Admission Procedures

2.4.1          New Owner Nomination

2.4.1.1                Nomination Criteria

In the event an owner leaves, the league will be asked to nominate new members. Any member of the league, including the commissioner, can nominate any number of new owners to fill open spots.  Each member is asked to consider whether a potential nominee meets some/all of the following suggested criteria:

·         Likely to remain in the league for a significant period of time

·         Multiple members of the league would say they know/are familiar with the nominee

·         Nominee follows the NFL and will strive to build/manage a successful team

·         Nominee is available for the upcoming draft date (if one has been set)

2.4.1.2                Nomination Period

The commissioner will decide on an appropriate nomination collection period based on time available to draft. This will typically be relatively short (e.g. 3 days).

2.4.1.3                Nomination Voting

The commissioner will administrate a league vote by email, in which the candidates will be ranked by league members, and the decision will be made via “instant run-off” procedures (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instant-runoff_voting).

Process will be repeated until all open league spots have been filled.

2.4.1.4                New Owner Keepers

Accepted new owners will be entitled to choose keepers from the team of the owner(s) that left the league. In the event that multiple owner spots need to be filled in a given offseason, the runoff voting process in section 2.4.1.3 will be used to establish a priority among new owners.  The owner with the top priority will be given the choice of all available teams, and this process will repeat until all owners have a team from which to draw their keepers.

3       Rosters

3.1  Roster Size

3.1.1          Starting Roster Positions

                                1 QB

                                2 RB

                                2 WR

                                1 UTIL (RB/WR/TE)

                                1 TE

                                1 K

                                1 Team DEF/SP

3.1.2          Bench Roster Positions

                                There will be 6 bench spots on each roster. 

3.1.3          Injured Reserve Roster Positions

During the course of the season, there will be two IR (Injured Reserve) roster spots open.  This will allow an owner to keep the rights to an injured player, but temporarily pick up a replacement. 

If an owner chooses to draft a player that is injured at the start of the season, they must later move that player to the IR, and pick up a replacement on the free agent market or through a trade.

3.2  Draft

3.2.1          Draft Format

The format will be an auction style draft, with earmarked keepers costing the same amount as was originally paid for them.   The draft budget will be $200 (prior to deducting keeper costs). 

 

Each team must draft or keep at least one player for each active roster spot (Starting Roster Positions or Bench Roster Positions).  That is, no player will be allowed to spend all of his money prior to filling his roster. 

3.2.2          Keeper Rules

3.2.2.1                Number of Keepers

Owners may choose to keep up to 4 players from his roster as of the end of the previous season.  Owners may choose to keep fewer than 4 players if they so choose.

3.2.2.2                Keeper Lock Date

All managers must communicate their keepers by setting them on the league site or communicating to the commissioner prior to the Keeper Lock Date.

Keeper lock date will be approximately 3 calendar days prior to the draft. 

3.2.2.3                Keeper Costs

The cost of the kept players will reduce the player’s available auction budget.

 

The cost of each keeper will be the midpoint between the drafted value and the ESPN Average Draft Value as of 1 week before the keeper lock date.  Costs will be rounded down to the nearest whole dollar. 

3.3  Roster Management/Player Moves, Adds, Drops, & Trades

3.3.1          Designating Starters

Starters and bench players can be set based on our chosen website’s rules and procedures (typically 30 minutes prior to the actual player’s NFL game begins). 

3.3.2          Free Agent Pickups

3.3.2.1                Free agent method

Free agent pickups will use an auction method. From the auction start time to the auction end time each week, each team may submit a blind bid on free agents on the league website. 

 

At the auction end, the highest bidder will get the player added to their roster.  The player that is dropped to accommodate the pickup is not available for pickup during that week (but will be the following week as part of the auction process).

3.3.2.1.1                       Auction Start & End Time

The auction start time will be at kickoff of each individual’s game.  The auction end time will be Thursday at 11 am Central Time.  After the auction end time, a new auction will begin, ending at 11 am Central Time each day until Sunday at 11 am Central Time. 

3.3.2.1.2                       Budget and Bidding

Free Agent auction bids may be in $1 increments, from $0 to the budget available for that owner. 

 

Auction budget will be $30 for the year. Price paid at auction will be the value used as the previous draft price for keeper purposes in the following draft.  (minimum $1).  

3.3.3          Trades

3.3.3.1                Trade Deadline

There will be a trade deadline prior to the start of the post-season. 

 

Trades can be proposed and processed at any time prior to the trade deadline. 

 

Trades will be automatically processed 1 day after they are accepted, or earlier by arrangement with the commissioner.  

 

For trades accepted less than 1 day prior to NFL games, reasonable attempts will be made by the commissioner to process the trade ahead of the games. 

 

It is the responsibility of the traders to communicate the processing of the trade, as well as the intentions for starting/sitting players after the trade in the event of tight timelines.  If those intentions are made clear, and the commissioner determines that the trade was made in reasonable time, rosters will be adjusted according to the instructions if necessary.

 

The trade deadline will be lifted following the resolution of the championship game to allow for trading of keepers during the off-season.

3.3.3.2                Auction budget trading

Prior to the keeper lock date (including during the prior season), auction budgets can be traded for keeper picks.

 

The trading parties must contact the commissioner to confirm the trade, and disclose the arrangement to the league via the league website’s message board.

4       Game Play

4.1  Schedule 

4.1.1          Regular Season Schedule

The week-to-week schedule will be randomly assigned by ESPN (or other league site). 

 

If there is an even number of teams in the league, every team will play each week. 

 

If the number teams is odd one team will be assigned a “bye” each week and will not get credit for a win or a loss during that week.  Every attempt will be made by the Commissioner prior to the season starting to get an even number of teams. 

4.1.2          Regular Season Duration

The regular season will last 13 weeks.

4.1.3          Playoff scheduling

4.1.3.1                Championship Bracket

In week 14, Seed 3 will play Seed 6, and Seed 4 will play Seed 5. 

 

In Week 15, Seed 1 will play the winner of the Seed 4/5 game, and Seed 2 will play the winner of the Seed 3/6 game. 

 

The championship will be played in week 16 by the winners of the week 15 playoff games.

4.1.3.2                Consolation Bracket

Teams that did not make the championship bracket or were previously eliminated from the championship bracket will be matched up according to the league website’s algorithm.

4.3  Post Season Seeding

4.3.1          Division Champions

Division Champions will earn seeds 1 and 2 in the playoffs.

 

Seeding between the two division champions will be determined following the tie-break procedures in this constitution.

4.3.2          Wild Cards

The top 4 teams that are not division winners will also earn playoff berths, and will be seeded 3-6 for the playoffs in order of tie-break procedures in this constitution.

4.4  Tie breakers

4.4.1          Determining Playoff Seeds in event of a tie

i)            Winning Percentage

ii)          Regular Season Points

iii)         Last Week Points (repeat week by week until winner is determined)

iv)         Coin Flip.

4.4.2          Determining Playoff Game Winners in event of a tie

i)            Total Points from starters

ii)          Bench Points

iii)         Points by position (starter) until winner is determined (order as listed in section 2.1)

iv)         Coin Flip.
