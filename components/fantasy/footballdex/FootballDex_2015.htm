FootballDex Fantasy League Rules and Information (Revised 12/10/2015)

1                   League Description & Overview

1.1                          League Size & Structure

The league will consist of 12 teams. There will be two divisions.

1.2                          Cost

The entry fee will be $50. Free agent pickups are free . Additionally, if the league decides at the draft to use a website that has an associated fee, the cost will be split between all teams equally.

1.3                          Payouts

Payout will total 100% of entry fees. Regular season points champion will get the pool of free agent money. With 12 teams, the total pool is $600.

1.4                          League Changes

Changes to the intent of anything in this constitution can only be made by an official league vote. Each team will have one vote, so co-owned teams (if any) will either split their vote equally, or will agree on a single choice.

 

Changes to the language, organization, content, or appearance of the constitution can be made by the Commissioner at any time, so long as the intent of anything existing previously in the constitution is not changed.

1.4.1        Pre-Season Changes

 

Prior to the start of a draft, any proposals will be considered for the upcoming year. A new proposal will be considered adopted if the proposal is approved by a super-majority of not less than two-thirds of teams in the league.

1.4.2        In Season Changes

After a draft has started, any changes to the rules that are to take effect for the remainder of the current season that must be approved by all active owners.

1.4.3        Unavoidable Changes

In the event that a change is required due to inability or impracticality of enforcing a rule (e.g. Website changes cause undue burden on commissioner, etc.), the Commissioner is responsible for communicating change options that will remedy the situation. Since rejecting all of the options does not effectively deal with the problem, a simple majority (or plurality for more than 2 options) will be used to decide the new approach.

1.4.4        Change Incorporation

Adopted changes will be written into the constitution by the Commissioner, and he or she has full discretion over the wording and organization of the change, so long as it matches the intent of what was voted upon.

1.5.2        Ownership Rules

 

Owners must be employed with Allianz Life in order to remain in the league. For purposes of determination, employment as of the time the draft date is set is sufficient for inclusion in a given season.

 

If an owner vacates their spot for an upcoming season, the commissioner will be responsible for replacing that owner and/or proposing a league restructuring. The following criteria will be used in the order stated to determine new entrants:

 

1.       Seniority within FootballDex (prior players receive priority for re-entry)

2.       Highest person on the official FootballDex Waiting List from the vacated spots division/Allianz department

3.       Highest person on the official FootballDex Waiting List regardless of Allianz department/division fit.

4.       If league openings remain after the prior criteria have been met, new applicants are considered with preference to:

a.        Division/League fit (e.g. If the division with an opening is entirely from one department, preference will be given to an employee from that department).

b.       Seniority within Allianz Life, rounded down to full years of service, based on official Peoplesoft hire date.

c.        Commissioner decision.

 

Individuals expressing interest in joining the league can be placed on the official FootballDex Waiting List to increase their chances of entry in future years.

2                   Team Structure

2.1                          Roster Size

Starting Rosters will include:

1 QB, 2 RB, 2 WR, 1 TE, 1 FLEX (RB/WR/TE), 1 K, 1 Team DEF/SP

There will be 6 bench spots on each roster.

2.2                          Roster Management

2.2.1        Starters/Non-starters

Starters and bench players can be chosen based on our chosen websites rule (typically 30 minutes prior to the actual players game begins).

2.2.2        Free Agent Pickups

Players can be acquired during the season by way of a Free Agent Auction. There will be an auction on Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, and Sunday at 10am CT (11am ET). Players requested will be part of the next available auction.

 

Auction budget will be $50 per team per season. You may bid $0 on a player, and he will not count against the budget (but may have an associated pickup cost according to the section describing actual costs).

2.2.3        Trades

Trade timing will work similarly to the Free Agent Pickup rules. There will be a trade deadline prior to the start of the post-season.

2.2.4        Cost for Pickups

Trades with other owners will be free. Each Free Agent pickup will cost $1. (See Section 1 for details on the cost/payout structure.)

2.2.5        Injured Reserve

Each team will have 1 Injured Reserve (IR) spot. Placing an injured player in this spot will allow for a pickup of 1 additional player at any position. This pickup will count against any pickup limits and associated costs as established elsewhere in this document.

3                   Draft

3.1.1        Format

To give everyone equal opportunity at each player, the draft will use an auction format.

3.1.1.1   Auction Budget

Each owner will have $200 of auction money, and must fill the roster, spending at least $1 on each player.

Example: For a 15 player roster, the most that could be spent on the first player is $186, leaving $1 for each of the other 14 players.

3.1.2        Restricted Free Agents (RFAs)

3.1.2.1   Number of RFAs

Each team will be allowed to designate 1 player from their prior years roster as a restricted free agent.

3.1.2.2   Process for RFAs

With the first nomination of the draft for each owner, the owner must nominate a player that was on their roster at the end of the prior year, was a free agent at the end of the prior year, or is currently a rookie in the NFL. If the player was a free agent or is currently a rookie, nothing special occurs (bidding will proceed, and the draft will continue once bidding is completed).

 

If the nominated player came from the nominators prior season roster, the player will be considered a restricted free agent. At that point, the bidding for that player will proceed as per normal process. Upon completion of the bidding, the nominator will have an opportunity to claim that player by matching the winning bid. No players nominated after the first round of the auction will be designated as restricted free agents (regardless if the owner chose to claim a player in the first round).

3.1.2.3   RFAs for new owners

Owners that did not participate in the prior season will inherit a roster from a prior owner for purposes of designating restricted free agents. In the event that there is more than one new owner to the league, the rules used to establish priority for league entry will also be used to establish the order of choice of prior rosters. (See Section 1.5.2).

3.1.3        Draft Theme Song

Each season, a draft theme song will be selected. This song will be played prior to the start of the draft, and occasionally throughout the draft at the discretion of the

3.1.3.1   Draft Theme Song Selection

Prior to the beginning of each draft, nominations will be solicited for an official draft theme song. This is intended to be an open nomination process, with little formality. Owners making a nomination must ensure that the nominated song is accessible enough to be played on demand during the draft should their song be selected.

 

Nominations will be taken one song at a time. Upon receiving a nomination, the Commissioner will hold a vote and either accept or reject the song as per the leagues Pre-Season Change rules. Upon rejection, the process will start again with the solicitation of a nominee. Upon acceptance of a song, the selection process will end for that seasons draft.

4                   Regular Season Play

4.1                          Schedule

The week-to-week schedule will be randomly assigned by whatever website we use to track this league. If there is an even number of teams in the league, every team will play each week. If the number teams is odd, each week, one team will be assigned a bye, and will not get credit for a win or a loss during that week. Every attempt will be made by the Commissioner prior to the season starting to get an even number of teams. The regular season will last 13 weeks.

5                   Post Season Play

5.1                          Seeding

5.1.1        Division Champions

Division Champions are defined as the team from each division with the top overall record during the regular season. The top 2 Division Champions (determined by overall record) will earn a bye in the first week of the playoffs (Week 14 of the Regular NFL season), and will earn seeds 1 and 2 in the playoffs. The third Division Champion will earn the 3 seed and will play against the weakest wildcard team.

5.1.2        Wild Cards

The top 3 teams that are not division winners will also earn playoff berths, and will be seeded 4-6 for the playoffs in order of winning percentage. In week 14, Seed 3 will play Seed 6, and Seed 4 will play Seed 5. In Week 15, Seed 1 will play the winner of the Seed 4/5 game, and Seed 2 will play the winner of the Seed 3/6 game. Then, of course, the championship will be played in week 16 with the only 2 remaining teams.

5.2                          Tie breakers

5.2.1        Determining Division Champions or Playoff Seeds in event of a tie

         Winning Percentage

         Regular Season Points

         Last Week Points (repeat week by week until winner is determined)

         Coin Flip

5.2.2        Determining Playoff Game Winners in event of a tie

         Total Points from starters

         Bench Points

         Points by position (starter) until winner is determined (order as listed in section 2.1)

         Coin Flip.

Any team who uses the default ESPN name for their team name as of Sunday of a given week will lose 20 points from their teams total for that week. Default is taken to mean any team whose name is Team [Last Name] where [Last Name] is the name of the owner of said team.

2015.6 FAAB Adjustment

ESPN

Each player will be locked at their teams scheduled gametime for a waiver period. During the waiver period, owners can submit a waiver claim and bid from their FAAB. At the end of the waiver period, the highest bidding owner will be awarded the player.

 

After the waiver period, all unclaimed players will become free agents until their next scheduled game, and will be awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.

2016.2 FAAB Structure

Justin Hanson

Proposal:

Each player will be locked at their teams scheduled gametime for a waiver period. During that waiver period, owners can submit a waiver claim and bid from their FAAB. At the end of the waiver period, the highest bidding owner will be awarded the player. If the highest bid was matched by two or more owners, the tie-break order is determined by reverse order of standings.

 

After the waiver period for a week is over, all unclaimed players will become free agents until their next scheduled game, allowing pickups on a first-come first-served basis.
